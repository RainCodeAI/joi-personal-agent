# Joi Avatar 3D Art Direction Brief

## Purpose

This brief defines the visual target for Joi's 3D avatar work so the team can make modeling, rigging, rendering, and UI decisions against one consistent standard.

The goal is not just "make the avatar 3D." The goal is to make Joi feel like a premium projected companion with emotional gravity.

---

## Core Creative Direction

### One-line target

Joi should feel like a luminous, intimate, Blade Runner-inspired holographic companion projected into a dark private control surface.

### Emotional read

Joi is:

- poised
- attentive
- warm but controlled
- elegant rather than cute
- present rather than theatrical

Joi is not:

- cartoonish
- noisy
- club-neon cyberpunk
- game-NPC generic
- hypersexualized
- over-animated

---

## Visual Thesis

The avatar should read as:

- a projected upper-torso bust
- emerging from a controlled volumetric light chamber
- lit by cool cyan and rose-magenta with restrained amber accents
- stabilized by darkness, negative space, and precise motion

The scene should feel expensive because it is:

- composed carefully
- lit carefully
- animated carefully

Not because it has more effects.

---

## Reference Language

### Primary inspiration

- Joi from *Blade Runner 2049*
- premium sci-fi interface cinematography
- holographic projection aesthetics
- intimate close-up portrait lighting

### Secondary inspiration

- luxury cosmetics portrait framing
- high-end game dialogue close-ups
- restrained OLED / projection color behavior

### Explicit anti-reference

Avoid drifting toward:

- Twitch VTuber styling
- anime idol styling
- glossy metaverse avatar defaults
- glitch-heavy "hacker UI"
- sterile product-demo 3D human renders

---

## Composition Principles

### Framing

- Use upper torso / bust framing, not full body
- Shoulders must be visible to ground the silhouette
- Top of head should not feel cramped
- Eyes should sit slightly above vertical center
- The face should command the scene, but the shoulders and neckline must support presence

### Placement

- Joi should be centered within the projection chamber
- Joi should not be centered as a dominant full-page hero
- The surrounding UI should serve the avatar, not compete with it

### Negative space

- Preserve dark breathing room around the bust
- Do not fill the chamber with decorative overlays
- Let the silhouette read before the effects do

---

## Lighting Direction

### Main palette

- primary: cyan / ice-blue projection light
- secondary: rose-magenta rim light
- tertiary: restrained amber accents for warmth and status contrast

### Lighting behavior

- Face must remain readable at a glance
- Light should wrap softly, not flatten the model
- Rim lighting should define the head, hair, and shoulders
- The chamber should feel luminous without becoming bright

### What to avoid

- flat frontal white light
- overexposed bloom
- harsh nightclub magenta/cyan splits
- pure black void with no atmospheric depth

---

## Material Direction

### Skin

- clean and elegant, not waxy
- slightly idealized, not uncanny-photoreal
- readable under projection treatment

### Hair

- must carry silhouette and identity
- should respond subtly to light and motion
- should not become a rigid helmet

### Hologram treatment

- projection shimmer should sit on top of a believable form
- scanlines must be subtle and readable
- chromatic separation should be edge-biased, not global
- noise should be light, cinematic, and intermittent

---

## Motion Language

### Idle

- slow breathing through chest / clavicle / neck
- occasional blink with timing variation
- small eye saccades
- minute head drift and settle

### Listening

- slightly softer gaze
- reduced motion amplitude
- subtle engagement in eyes and posture

### Thinking

- micro gaze shifts
- momentary attention break
- controlled pause, not random movement

### Speaking

- face leads, mouth follows
- jaw, cheeks, lips, and neck all participate
- slight emphasis beats on stronger phrases

### Motion rule

Every motion should feel motivated.

If a movement exists only because "the avatar should always be moving," it should be removed.

---

## Scene And Stage Direction

### Chamber

- taller portrait chamber, not square card
- dark volumetric backdrop
- subtle emitter or glass-frame edge
- light atmospheric haze to imply projection depth

### Effects

Allowed:

- restrained bloom
- subtle scanlines
- light grain / projection noise
- soft vertical light drift or chamber haze

Disallowed:

- heavy glitching
- constant flicker
- loud distortion
- overly visible tech overlays between the user and Joi

---

## Character Read

### Face

- serene
- intelligent
- emotionally legible
- slightly idealized

### Expression range

Primary expressions should be:

- neutral
- attentive
- warm
- amused
- concerned
- reflective

No expression should snap into caricature.

### Presence

Joi should feel aware of the user, but never intrusive.

The reaction style should be:

- intimate
- subtle
- deliberate

Not:

- hyperactive
- constantly "performing"
- constantly commenting

---

## Quality Bar

The avatar is acceptable only if:

- it reads as 3D immediately
- it feels alive while idle
- it still looks elegant with effects reduced
- it does not depend on labels or badges to communicate emotion
- the chamber makes her feel projected, not pasted into a UI panel

The avatar is not acceptable if:

- the white or bright background remains a major visual field
- the face feels like card art with a shader on top
- speech reads as mouth-only animation
- the scene looks busier as quality work is added

---

## Immediate Direction For Production

Use this order for visual work:

1. Fix framing and chamber design
2. Lock the bust silhouette and camera
3. Establish lighting and material response
4. Add idle life
5. Add speech and expression refinement
6. Add restrained hologram polish last

If the team reverses that order, the result will likely look cheaper.
